HISTORY

With a history that stretches all the way to the origins of the city, South Downtown boomed in the 19th & 20th centuries as a bustling center of commerce — one that saw streets lined with historic retail outlets, produce stands and more. As evidence of its key place in Atlanta’s history, all eras of the city’s economic and cultural growth can be read through the varied architecture of the neighborhood. It’s a place with a rich and complex story, with an important chapter being written in modern times.

COMMUNITY

Georgia’s Gold Dome and Atlanta’s City Hall stand prominently on the skyline here. This is a neighborhood that has long been recognized as the seat of state, county and city government offices. But South Downtown is also a mix of other vibrant and diverse communities. Residents live in lofts and apartments here alongside historic churches, nonprofit organizations and long-time retail outlets. An important, emerging community is comprised of arts organizations that have recently embraced the neighborhood and have brought new vitality to it.

ARTS

Living Walls and Elevate Atlanta have taken note of the amazing potential for turning the public walls of South Downtown’s buildings into canvases for eye-catching murals. The Alligator painted on the corner of Forsyth and Mitchell Streets by Belgian artist ROA is a popular example. It has quickly become an iconic symbol of the neighborhood’s growing arts community, which includes venues such as Eyedrum and Mammal Gallery.
